Artifacts

Arca Kala

Kala head is a sort of door guardian that is placed at the door of the candi, is one of the common parts in Hindu-Buddhist temples. It was discovered at Site 50, the Candi Bendang Dalam, now in the Bujang Valley Archaeological Museum.

Relics box

Relics box is a type of container used to places the relics or the pious (penipih). Relics are usually placed in certain parts of the temple for religious and veneration purpose. Relics are also known as stone coffins or stone box.

Clay pot

Mambong clay pot with lid made of clay with a combination of bamboo shoots. However another stemmed pitcher with a combination of rhombus (fish nets). The letter X and moles patterns is using the sticking and stamping technique.

Shiva lingam

A lingam, is an abstract or aniconic representation of the Hindu god Shiva in Shaivism. The yoni, its feminine counterpart, consisting of a flat element, horizontal compared to the vertical lingam, and designed to allow liquid offerings to drain away for collection.

Buddhagupta Inscription

The inscription made of slaty type stone/shale. This insription was discovered by Colonel Sir James Low in 1834 associated with the discovery of the ruins of the candi in Kampung Sireh Guar Kepah Seberang Perai. There is a carved image of a Stupa and Pallava script

Arca Ganesha

This Ganesha statue is made of granite, dating back to the sixth century AD.Found in Site 4 in Kampung Sungai Batu, Kedah. Ganesha s one of the best-known and most worshipped deities in the Hindu pantheon
